How to Incorporate a Variable Capital Company (VCC) in Singapore (2026 Guide)

📅 July 2026 ⏱ 11 min read ✍️ Karman Editorial Team

The Variable Capital Company (VCC) is Singapore's purpose-built fund structure, introduced under the Variable Capital Companies Act 2018. Unlike a standard Pte Ltd, the VCC can vary its share capital freely, maintain a private register of members, and house multiple sub-funds under a single entity - making it the dominant vehicle for Singapore-based fund managers across private equity, hedge funds, real estate, and family offices.

This guide walks through the complete VCC incorporation process - from eligibility to ACRA registration to first investor close.

Step 1: Confirm Eligibility - The Fund Manager Requirement

The single most important eligibility condition: a VCC must at all times be managed by an MAS-regulated fund manager. The fund manager must hold one of the following:

  • A Capital Markets Services (CMS) licence from MAS for fund management under the Securities and Futures Act, or
  • A Registered Fund Management Company (RFMC) status (for managers with AUM below S$250 million and fewer than 30 qualified investors), or
  • An exemption under SFA Section 99(1) - e.g., licensed banks, licensed insurers, or single family offices managing exclusively related-party capital
Common mistake: Founders sometimes try to incorporate a VCC before their fund management entity is licensed. ACRA will require confirmation of the fund manager's regulatory status before approving the VCC. Secure or apply for your CMS licence or RFMC status first.

Step 2: Decide on Structure - Standalone or Umbrella

Before filing, decide whether your VCC will be:

  • Standalone: A single fund entity with one pool of assets and investors. Simpler to administer; right for a single-strategy fund.
  • Umbrella: A single corporate entity housing two or more sub-funds, each legally segregated. Better for multi-strategy managers or those expecting to launch additional funds.

This decision affects your constitution drafting and cannot be easily changed post-incorporation without ACRA approval. If in doubt, incorporate as an umbrella - the incremental cost is minimal and it preserves flexibility. Full comparison →

Step 3: Draft the VCC Constitution

Unlike a standard Pte Ltd (which can use a template constitution), a VCC constitution must be carefully tailored. It must include:

  • The VCC's name and registered office address
  • The investment objective and permitted asset classes
  • Share capital mechanics - how shares are issued, redeemed, and cancelled
  • NAV calculation methodology and frequency
  • Sub-fund creation and segregation provisions (for umbrella VCCs)
  • Cost allocation policy across sub-funds
  • Investor rights - subscription, redemption, transfer, and voting
  • Director appointment, removal, and quorum requirements
  • Winding-up and liquidation provisions

The constitution is a legal document and should be drafted by a Singapore fund lawyer. ACRA will review it as part of the incorporation application and may request amendments.

Step 5: File with ACRA via BizFile+

The VCC incorporation is filed through ACRA's BizFile+ portal by an ACRA Registered Filing Agent (RFA). The filing package includes:

  • Completed VCC incorporation application form
  • Proposed VCC constitution (reviewed and signed)
  • Consent of directors and company secretary
  • Confirmation of fund manager's MAS regulatory status
  • KYC documents for all directors and controllers
  • Registered office address in Singapore

ACRA filing fee: S$3,000 for a new VCC (significantly higher than a standard Pte Ltd at S$315).

ACRA typically processes complete applications within 3–5 business days. Upon approval, ACRA issues a Certificate of Incorporation and assigns a UEN to the VCC.

Sub-fund registration: For umbrella VCCs, each sub-fund must also be registered separately with ACRA after the umbrella entity is incorporated. The sub-fund registration fee is S$400 per sub-fund.

Step 6: Apply for MAS Tax Incentive (13O or 13U)

Most VCCs apply for either the Section 13O (Onshore Fund Tax Exemption) or Section 13U (Enhanced Tier Fund Tax Exemption) to shield investment income from Singapore corporate tax. This application is separate from ACRA incorporation and is submitted to MAS/EDB.

Step 7: Open Bank Accounts and Set Up Custody

After receiving the ACRA Certificate of Incorporation:

  • Open a corporate bank account for the VCC (and per sub-fund if umbrella)
  • Execute a formal custody agreement with your appointed custodian
  • Set up your fund administrator's systems with the VCC's share register format
  • Execute subscription agreements and term sheets for first investors

Bank account opening for VCCs typically takes 4–12 weeks depending on the institution and the fund's investor profile. Major Singapore banks (DBS, OCBC, UOB) and international private banks all accept VCC accounts.

Updated May 2026

Singapore's Variable Capital Company (VCC) framework continues to expand rapidly, with MAS reporting over 1,100 registered VCCs as of Q1 2026. The 2026 Budget extended the VCC Grant (up to S$150,000 co-funding) through 2028 and simplified onboarding for family offices under the 13O and 13U tax incentive schemes. If you are evaluating a fund structure, Singapore's VCC remains the most tax-efficient and administratively flexible option in Asia.

Key Takeaways (Updated July 2026)

The Variable Capital Company remains Singapore's purpose-built fund vehicle, and its core advantages are unchanged in 2026: a single VCC can house multiple sub-funds with ring-fenced assets and liabilities, capital can be issued and redeemed at net asset value without the rigidity of ordinary share capital, and the structure sits comfortably alongside Singapore's 13O and 13U tax incentives. For fund managers and family offices, it is the natural home for both open-ended and closed-end strategies.

Two things are non-negotiable when you incorporate a VCC. First, it must be managed by a permissible fund manager - typically a MAS-licensed or registered entity, or an exempt manager - so the manager arrangement needs to be settled before you file. Second, every VCC must appoint an approved fund administrator and comply with AML/CFT obligations under MAS oversight. Getting the manager, administrator, and custodian lined up in the right order is what determines your timeline.

Because the VCC touches fund management licensing, tax incentives, and fund administration all at once, the cleanest path is to sequence those decisions before incorporation rather than after.
