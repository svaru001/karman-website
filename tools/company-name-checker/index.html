ACRA naming rules

What ACRA looks for when reviewing names

Entity suffix required

A Singapore private limited company must end with "Pte. Ltd.", "Pte Ltd", or "Private Limited". Public companies use "Ltd" or "Limited". LLPs use "LLP". The suffix is part of the legal name - using a different one is grounds for rejection.

Uniqueness test

ACRA rejects names that are identical to or "too similar" to existing entities. Differences in punctuation, articles (The, A), or generic words don't count - "Tech Co" is too similar to "Tech Company". Check Bizfile's directory before reserving.

Restricted & prohibited words

Words like "Bank", "Insurance", "School", "Medical", "Law" require approval from MAS, MOE, MOH, or Law Society. "Singapore", "National", "ASEAN", "Royal" are sensitive and need ACRA discretion. "Temasek", "Government" - prohibited outright.

Approval timeline

Clean names approve in ~15 minutes through Bizfile. Names with restricted words triggering external referral (MAS, MOE, etc.) take 2-8 weeks. Once approved, your name is reserved for 120 days - incorporate within that window or reapply.
